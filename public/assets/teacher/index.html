What is Version Control?
Point 1 of 5
Big picture overview of why we use Git before touching any commands.
1
Version control keeps a complete history of changes in your project.
2
You can safely roll back when experiments or refactors break things.
3
Multiple developers can work on the same files without overwriting each other.
4
Every change is tagged with who made it and why, via commit messages.
5
Your repository lives on your own machine, so you are not tied to one server.
